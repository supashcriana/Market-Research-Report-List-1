<p><p>The medical thermometer market encompasses various types, including mercury-in-glass thermometers, digital thermometers, infrared thermometers, and disposable thermometers. Mercury-in-glass thermometers offer traditional accuracy but pose safety risks. Digital thermometers provide quick and precise readings, appealing for home use and clinical settings. Infrared thermometers, often used for non-contact measurements, are popular in crowded or sensitive environments. Disposable thermometers ensure hygiene and prevent cross-contamination, making them ideal for clinical settings, especially in pandemic conditions or during mass screenings.</p></p>

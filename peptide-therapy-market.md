<p><p>Peptide therapy encompasses various routes of administration, each with distinct characteristics and applications. The parenteral route involves injections, allowing for rapid absorption and effectiveness, commonly used for therapeutic peptides. The mucosal route includes administration via nasal or oral mucosa, facilitating faster onset of action. The oral route offers convenience but faces challenges with peptide stability and absorption. Lastly, the transdermal route uses skin patches for gradual delivery, promoting sustained release while minimizing systemic side effects. Each route caters to specific patient needs and therapeutic goals.</p></p>

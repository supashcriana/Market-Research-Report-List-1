<p><p>The 1,4-Butanediol market comprises several production processes. The Reppe process, utilizing acetylene and formaldehyde, is a traditional method. The Davy process uses butadiene to produce 1,4-butanediol through hydrogenation. The Butadiene process also leverages butadiene, focusing on cost efficiency and simplicity. Lastly, the Propylene oxide process involves converting propylene oxide to 1,4-butanediol, offering an alternative route. Each method's market is influenced by raw material availability, production costs, and demand in industries such as plastics and solvents.</p></p>

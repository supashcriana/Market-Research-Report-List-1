<p><p>Particle size analyzing equipment encompasses several market types, each utilizing distinct methods. Laser diffraction measures particle size based on light scattering patterns. Dynamic light scattering analyzes the scattering of laser light to determine particle size in a suspension. Imaging analysis captures and measures particles using high-resolution cameras. The Coulter principle counts particles by measuring changes in electrical resistance. Nanoparticle tracking analysis visualizes and tracks individual nanoparticles in a fluid. Other methods may include sieve analysis and sedimentation techniques, catering to diverse particle characterization needs.</p></p>

<p><p>AM (Amplitude Modulation) and FM (Frequency Modulation) radio are two fundamental broadcasting technologies for delivering audio content over the airwaves. AM radio transmits signals by varying the amplitude of the carrier wave, making it effective for long-distance transmission, especially during nighttime. FM radio, on the other hand, modulates the frequency of the carrier wave, resulting in higher sound quality and reduced interference, making it more popular for music transmission.</p><p>The AM and FM radio market is experiencing dynamic growth, driven by advancements in technology and an increasing demand for diverse audio content. The integration of digital platforms has enhanced listener engagement and expanded the audience reach. Additionally, the rise of internet radio and streaming services has prompted traditional radio stations to innovate and adapt, incorporating digital broadcasts and online streaming to retain listeners.</p><p>As consumers increasingly seek localized and relevant content, radio remains a trusted source for news, music, and entertainment.

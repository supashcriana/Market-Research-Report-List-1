<p><p>The fish sauce market is primarily divided into two types: traditional and industrial fish sauce. Traditional fish sauce is typically handmade using artisanal methods, often involving fermentation of seafood for extended periods. This type emphasizes natural flavors and regional characteristics, appealing to consumers seeking authenticity. In contrast, industrial fish sauce is mass-produced, utilizing mechanized processes that ensure consistency and lower costs. This version usually contains additives for enhanced flavor and preservation, targeting a broader market with diverse culinary needs.</p></p>
